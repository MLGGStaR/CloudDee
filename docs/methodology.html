Methodology

How CloudDee turns federal public records into watchable case files. Last updated: May 13, 2026.

This page documents the editorial pipeline behind every CloudDee video. Transparency about how the channel is produced is part of the editorial standards we publish under.

1. Ingestion

Once per day, an automated job pulls newly released documents from five US Government sources of record:

  • NTSB CAROL public API (aviation accident reports)
  • NTSB CAROL public API (marine accident reports)
  • justice.gov press releases (RSS feed)
  • courtlistener.com (US federal court opinions and dockets, via the Free Law Project's free API)
  • sec.gov EDGAR full-text search (8-K filings and litigation releases)

Only public documents published in the last 7 days are considered. Each record is stored with its source URL, publication timestamp, title, and a normalized plain-text body for downstream processing.

2. Scoring

Each new record is evaluated by an AI language model under a strict editorial rubric. The model produces three independent scores from 1 to 10:

Drama — how compelling the underlying facts are to a general audience. A multimillion-dollar fraud scores high; a procedural docket entry scores low.
Novelty — how unusual the case is. Routine fender-bender investigations score low; first-of-its-kind charges or unusual fact patterns score high.
Visualizability — how well the events can be evoked through stock imagery and AI illustration. A plane crash scores high; an abstract regulatory dispute scores low.

The model also produces structured safety flags: whether the case is sealed, whether minors are identifiably involved, whether the matter is primarily about sexual violence against an identifiable victim, and whether the source materials raise defamation risk. Any positive flag excludes the record from production.

3. Selection

Records above the quality floor (a combined score of at least 10 out of 30, excluding flagged records) become candidates. From this pool the system picks:

  • One long-form documentary (5–10 minutes), the top-ranked unproduced record
  • Three Shorts (under 60 seconds each), the next candidates from the same pool — one a recap of the long-form, two standalone

4. Script writing

For each selected record, an AI language model writes the narration under a per-source-type editorial system prompt (one for aviation, one for federal court, one for SEC enforcement). The prompt enforces:

  • Presumption of innocence framing for unconvicted parties
  • Source citation by domain only ("data.ntsb.gov"), never by full URL or case number, to keep narration listenable
  • Stable scene structure (hook, setup, incident, investigation, takeaway, closing) so the editor can apply music and visual cues consistently
  • Variation requirements on the hook so videos don't open the same way every day

5. Voicing

The script is synthesized to audio using OpenAI's TTS-HD model. Four narrator voices rotate per record (chosen deterministically from the record's identifier) so the channel doesn't sound like one relentless monologue. The narration is then transcribed back to time-aligned captions using OpenAI's Whisper model, so on-screen subtitles match the actual delivery rather than the script.

6. Visuals

Each scene's b-roll comes first from Pexels (licensed stock photography and video, filtered to minimum 1080p resolution). Where stock returns nothing relevant — a recreated cockpit scene, a specific aircraft model — the system falls back to AI-generated imagery (OpenAI gpt-image-1). Both layers are composited with Ken Burns motion in FFmpeg.

7. Mastering and publishing

Audio is normalized to broadcast-standard loudness (−16 LUFS), subtitles are burned in, an outro card with the channel branding is appended, and the final 1080p (or 1080×1920 vertical for Shorts) mp4 is uploaded to YouTube via the YouTube Data API. Captions are uploaded separately as an SRT track for accessibility. A channel-owner comment with the related long-form URL is posted on every Short.

8. Disclosure

Every video upload includes a written disclosure in its description stating that narration is AI-synthesized and some imagery is AI-generated. The "altered or synthetic content" metadata flag is set on every upload. We do not impersonate any real person's voice or likeness.

9. Corrections

If a video contains a factual error, we accept correction requests at thememesofdoom@gmail.com. Corrections are processed within 7 business days and applied via description edits, pinned correction comments, or video removal depending on severity. We do not silently rewrite uploaded content.
